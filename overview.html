OVERVIEW

Private Enterprise, Kenya Government, Kakamega County Govt, and Athletic Kenya Ltd (AK) To
Start/Entrench long Term-program in Short Distance Running To Identify & Nurture Talent.

I, Dr. Makiazi along with friends, investors, advisers, and well-wishers, propose that we
form a partnership in the Western Region to start an annual running event by the name of
Kakamega Relays in which Kakamega stadium will host Short races in form of relays to identify
and nurture talents from the grassroots. As you are all aware, Western region has produced the
majority of short distance runners in Kenya over the years but the region lacks proper
framework of isolating talent and even when talent is identified or when a potential gold medal
winner stands out, we have no mechanism of helping that boy or the girl realize his/her full
potential. This should not be happening in the 21 st century especially since the world is inter-
connected and help is usually available for best performers. We just need to come together and
brainstorm. Together, there is so much we can do.

Athletics Kenya (AK) is very familiar with the relays system. They send athletes to relay races
across the world every year. Unfortunately, AK Kenya send athletes predominantly to long
distant races with a few exceptions. We can use Kakamega Relays to fill the cap in short distance
running.

Kakamega relays will focus on team relay running and individual running in 100M, 200M, 400M,
4x100M, 4x400M and field events notably, Javelin, Short Put, Discuss, Long Jump, Hurdles, Triple
Jump, and high Jump and Hammer. We envision a carnivore-like event that will bring all kinds of
people from everywhere to Bukhungu stadium to witness a two/three day of fine short/middle
distance running and field events.

Ideally, we shall have participants from age 6 years to 80 years age groups participating. We
shall encourage and incentivize institutions participations i.e. Primary schools, high schools,
colleges, athletic clubs, and individual entries, leaders, politicians, doctors, lawyers, mama
mbogas, Makangas, matautu drivers, govt departments, military, police etc. As long as they are
organized as a relay team, we shall have them on the track running.

We plan to give out real gifts, presents, or prices to winners as incentives for participation. We
want to thoroughly entertain Kenyans each year with this FREE event of relay running in
Bukhungu Stadium.

We shall endeavor to avoid events, schedules or programs that may overlap or conflict with
Athletics Kenya program. Ultimately, the aim is to achieve a national goal of raising world
beating teams in short distances and field events for the next hundreds of years.

The world needs to know that Kenyans are for real and that we are not just a country of long-
distance running composed of kids who benefitted from running five kilometers to school at a
young age.

One of the major relay events in the world is the ‘Penn Relays’ hosted in the city of Philadelphia,
Pennsylvania’ USA around April every year. I have personally attended many Penn Relays. It is a
breath-taking event, and oh yes, Kenyan athletes perform wonderful every year. To be at Penn
Relays with a Kenyan flag on your shoulders and with Kenyan athletes on track performing is the
next best thing to being in Heaven. You cry, you laugh, you are mobbed, you are congratulated
and you feel on top of the world, at least for that day. Kenyan Athletes NEVER disappoint in long
distance. Meanwhile, the USA and Jamaican axis at Penn Relays in short distance is simply
Angelic. The rivalry, the performance, between these two countries make the world stop for a
moment when they two are going against each other on the track. I have always thought Kenya
should be introduced in their mix. We, as a nation, have the athletics pedigree. We need to
follow AK roadmap to start beating the world in short distance running and field events too. I
am convinced Kakamega relay is the vehicle to achieve that goal.

This idea of Kakamega Relays has been in the works since 2014. The name Kakamega Relays is
officially registered with registrar of companies. At one time we bought some equipment for
relay running, printed some T-shirts and I approached Nakumatt and Tuskys for sponsorship and
they agreed in principle but then we got distracted. This time around we determined to get
Kakamega relays off the ground for the sake of future generations. we want to contribute, even
in a small way, in making Kenya the greatest athletics country God has ever created.
